IoT Storage Box

It works by IoT devices visiting an url endpoint, providing the data and api key that you set yourself. There is also an web instance you can use free of charge.

Universal IoT Development Board

One of my biggest projects was developing of my product called: "Universal IoT Development Board."

At that time, board consisted of all electronic parts in one simple package, that was easy to use, to allow any user, even without any programming experience to quickly got their own IoT thing up in the cloud.

I made all Electronic plans, manufactured board files, ordered the blank board from China and populated it all by myself.

At that time, the board was based on ESP8266 chip, and it included:

  • SMPS Power Supply
  • 16 Digital IO Ports
  • 6 ADC Ports
  • 5 DAC Outputs
  • RTC Clock
  • Optional HC-05 Bluetooth module
  • Optional DHT22 Temperature and Humidity Sensor

...all packed in small form factor of 100*60mm.

That board was probably my biggest project I have completed so far. All my knowledge and skills were recognized. So, the board granted me multiple awards in the field:

  1. Award for best Final Dissertation on the topic "Universal IoT Development Board", Slavonski Brod,Croatia.May 2018.
  2. 1st place for "Universal IoT Development Board" at regional Croatian contest "Mladi @ Inovacije 2018"
  3. 2nd place for "Universal IoT Development Board" at "Science Fair Ivanić grad 2018"
  4. 3rd place for "Universal IoT Development Board" at Inter-regional contest at University of Zagreb, Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computing,2018
Arduino RC Car

Back when I was at my high school in Croatia, I have designed simple RC Controlled Vehicle called simply "Arduino RC Car." I created it to compete at the Croatian science fair "Science Fair Ivanić Grad, 2017." 

Car consisted of 4WD electric system and was fully controllable over the app on any Android device. The range was decent and battery life was good enough to last through all 2 days of constant use.

That project granted me 3rd place bronze medal

Robo Challenge

During my high school in second grade I was part of “Robo Challenge” project that was held in my school back in Croatia.

Aim of the project was to introduce world of electronic and applied Software Engineering to young students like me at that time. The project was separated into two main parts. The first part was to assemble a robotic vehicle using appropriate tools, schematics and tools. The second part was to program that vehicle For it to do certain tasks like avoiding an obstacle, follow the line on the floor, or to enable remote control.

My colleagues and I were very successful at that project and we have completed out tasks, and additional ones in time span of 2 weeks.

This project is one of my favourites because at that time, I was only 15 years old, and this was my first big step into the world of programming.
